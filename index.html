Difference between rem,em,vh,vw,px.

rem : relative to root element

example of span with font-size : 2rem

I am a span and my font size is 2 rem i.e i will always have twice the font size of root element which is 16 px by default

em : relative to parent element

This div has a font-size of 20 px and hence the span should have 40 px as font size (=2em)
I am a span and my font size is 2 em i.e i will always have twice the font size of parent element(div) which is 20 px in this case

vh : relative to height of view port where 1vh = 1% of height of view port

Resize the height of window to see effect

vw : relative to width of view port where 1vh = 1% of width of view port

Resize the width of window to see effect

px : fixed size

I have a fixed font-size of 10px

Demo specifying difference between css combinators (>,+,~)

Explaining Child combinator (>)

I will be sleected as i am inside the div

I will also be selected as i am inside the div

I am outside the div so wont be selected


Explaining Adjacent sibling combinator(+)

I will not be selected as i am inside the div hence its child and not sibling

I am outside the div and its sibling and immdiately following it so will be selected

I am also outside the div and its sibling but do not immidiately following it so wont be selected


Explaining General sibling combinator(~)

I will not be selected as i am inside the div hence its child and not sibling

I am outside the div and its sibling so will be selected

I am also outside the div and its sibling so will be selected
